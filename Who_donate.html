WHO CAN DONATE BLOOD?

You are eligible to donate blood if you are in good health, weigh at least 110 pounds and are 17 years or older.

You are not eligible to donate blood if you:

* Have ever used self-injected drugs (non-prescription)
* Had hepatitis
* Are in a high-risk group for AIDS

Acceptable as long as your blood pressure is below 180 systolic (first number) and below 100 diastolic (second number) at the time of donation. Medications for high blood pressure do not disqualify you from donating.

Any healthy adult, both male and female, can donate blood once in every three months. Good health of the donor must be fully ensured. The universally accepted criteria for donor selection are:

1. Age between 18 and 60 years.
2. Hemoglobin : not less than 12.5 g/dL.
3. Pulse : between 50 and 100/minute with no irregularities.
4. Blood Pressure : Systolic 90 : 180 mm Hg and Diastolic 50 : 100 mm Hg.
5. Temperature : Normal (oral temperature not exceeding 37.5 degree C).
6. Body weight : not less than 46 Kg.
